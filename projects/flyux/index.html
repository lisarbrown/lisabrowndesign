FlyUX

UX/UI

Brief

For my Diploma in UX Design at the UX Design Institute, I was tasked with creating an interactive mobile app prototype to improve the online experience for the users of a fictional airline called Fly UX. To carry out this project, I implemented the following UX Design process:

The main goal of this project was to streamline and improve how customers search, selected and secured their booking online.

Research

The first stage in this project was research. I carried out both qualitative and quantitative research to gain insight to how customers were currently interacting with the online process and to understand what competitors were doing in this market. My research consisted of:

  • Online survey
  • Competitor benchmarking
  • User testing (current process)
  • Competitor user testing

Online survey

I created an online survey which was circulated through social media to gather insight from the general public. The questions covered: how users are currently booking their trips, how successful they usually are with their booking and how they find the experience in general. I was able to collect a large amount of data on the general consensus of users typical pain points and positives from the process such as frustration of price changes and the preference of having a clear layout to search with.

View online survey PDF

Competitive bench marking

Following the online survey, I went on to review competitors booking processes to understand best practice in the industry. Common UX features includes having the calendar appear when selecting dates allowing the user to see exactly what the dates and days are, another UX feature would be the rotate scrolling bar when selecting number of passengers instead of having to type them in individually.

View Competitive bench marking PDF

Customer User Testing

I watched interviews of users using the FlyUX booking system. During the user testing, I created notes highlighting positives and negatives that the users were coming across. This allowed me to identify areas where the UX could be improved by highlighting challenges that the customers were facing which was potentially causing them to abandon their booking.

View note taking PDF

Competitor User Testing

I conducted my own user testing, asking users to go through the booking process on two separate competitor apps (British Airways and RyanAir). These sessions were recorded (with the users content) and allowed me to again identify, positives and negatives in the users journey as they navigated through the apps.

Analysis

After going through the research stage, I moved on to the next stage of the UX design process, which is the analysis.

Affinity diagram

Collaborating with my colleague, we created an affinity diagram. We went through my research notes to pull out all the positives, negatives and other key points, and wrote them down on post-its. We then sorted them into groups from commons patterns that we found, to which we named each group appropriately.This allowed us to clearly see the areas that needed to be focused on for improving in the booking process.

View note taking PDF

Customer journey map

In the customer journey map, I split the booking process into 8 sections and then put in the notes above into 3 categories (goals, behaviours and pain points) under each of the 8 sections. This allowed me to gain an understanding of where the main pain points were in the customer journey and identify which stages of the journey to prioritise making improvements in to have the most impact overall.

View Customer journey PDF

What I learnt overall

Following the research and analysis stage I was able to see the biggest pain point in the process was the flight results listing as many users found it difficult to understand the pricing for the best deal or to see clearly which flights were direct or had stops. I prioritised this feature to be improved and decided to have “direct flight” in bold and clearly marked to allow these flights to stand out from the list. I also included a “direct flight” filter, for the user to have only direct flights as an option. Finally, I included a feature which would allow the user to compare selected flight to help them with deciding which flight would suit them best.

Wireframe

After reviewing the low fidelity prototype, I started to create more detailed screens using sketch to define the visual of the screen states. I then created a wireframe going into fine details of every screen state showing what the developers would require in order to build the flight booking process accurately.
